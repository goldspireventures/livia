# Outreach Script Kit — Design Partners (v1)

**Owner:** founder · **Status:** v1, May 2026

Five scripts the founder uses to convert a stranger into a design partner. Founder voice — calm, specific, not SDR-ish. Each script ≤ 120 words. Read each one aloud before sending — if it doesn't sound like a person, rewrite it.

**Merge fields used throughout:** `{{first_name}}`, `{{shop_name}}`, `{{neighbourhood}}`, `{{specific_observation}}`, `{{founder_first_name}}`, `{{mutual_first_name}}` (warm-intro only).

---

## 1. Instagram DM — three variants by vertical

### 1a. Barber

> Hey {{first_name}} — saw {{shop_name}} on the way through {{neighbourhood}} this week, your chairs are unreal.
>
> I'm building a tool called Livia. The bit that might be useful to you: when someone DMs you at 11pm asking for a slot tomorrow, Livia replies for you with real availability, takes the booking, and you wake up to a confirmed appointment instead of a cold conversation.
>
> I'm onboarding 10 Dublin shops free for the first three months in exchange for honest feedback. No commitment. Want me to set you up this week?
>
> — {{founder_first_name}}

### 1b. Nails / lashes

> Hi {{first_name}} — your {{specific_observation}} stopped me scrolling.
>
> Quick question: how many of the DMs you get on a Friday night actually turn into bookings? I'm working on something called Livia that handles those DMs for you — it knows your prices, your slots, and your house rules, and it books people into your calendar without you ever picking up the phone.
>
> Looking for 10 Dublin studios to try it free for three months. Curious to know if {{shop_name}} would be one of them.
>
> — {{founder_first_name}}

### 1c. Tattoo / dental

> Hey {{first_name}} — I'm reaching out because {{shop_name}} clearly takes consultations seriously, and that's the exact use case I'm trying to learn from.
>
> I'm building Livia — a quiet assistant that triages new enquiries, asks the qualifier questions you'd ask anyway, and only puts the genuinely-ready ones on your calendar. Less time on tyre-kicker DMs, more time at the chair.
>
> Onboarding 10 Dublin practices free for three months. Would you be up for a 15-minute call this week to see if it'd actually be useful?
>
> — {{founder_first_name}}

## 2. In-person walk-in pitch (60 seconds spoken)

> Hi — sorry to interrupt, one minute promise. I'm {{founder_first_name}}, based around {{neighbourhood}}. I built something called Livia. When someone DMs you on Instagram or texts after hours, Livia replies on your behalf — knows your prices, your slots, your house rules — and books them in. You see every conversation and take over any time.
>
> Looking for ten Dublin shops to try it free for three months in exchange for honest feedback. No contracts, no card. Could I leave you my card and follow up next week?

> **Subject:** Quick idea for {{shop_name}}

It answers DMs and texts on your behalf — knows your prices, your slots, your house rules — and turns evening enquiries into morning bookings while you sleep. > {{founder_first_name}}

## 4. Follow-up nudge — after 3 days no reply

>
> No pressure at all. If now isn't the right week I'll happily try again next month. If you'd rather I stop, just say "not for me" and I'll never message you again — promise.
>
> Either way, thanks for the time.
>
> — {{founder_first_name}}

## Voice rules (apply to every script before sending)

1. **No "I hope this email finds you well."** Ever.
2. **No "synergy," "leverage," "solution," "platform," "ROI," "best-in-class."**
3. **First sentence proves you've actually looked at the shop.** Generic = delete.
4. **One ask per message.** A reply *or* a meeting, never both.
5. **End with an out.** "Just say not for me" beats any close.
6. **Read aloud before sending.** If you wouldn't say it to a friend across a table, rewrite it.
