# Founding story — F10

**Status:** F10 (2026-05-07). The story we tell hires, investors, design partners, customers. Why now. Why us. Why Ireland.

## Why now

Three convergences:

1. **Frontier LLMs crossed the threshold for operator-grade reliability in late 2024 / early 2025.** Before that, AI in service-business software was a chatbot bolted on. After that, an agent could plausibly hold context across a Tuesday morning, draft a refund decision a Manager would actually use, run a voice line a regular customer would prefer over a callback. The threshold matters; we're building on the right side of it.

2. **The European appointment-software incumbents (Phorest, Treatwell, Fresha, Booksy, Mindbody) all bolted AI features onto pre-AI architectures in 2024–2025.** None re-architected. Bolt-ons feel like bolt-ons. The category has space for an AI-native challenger that re-thinks the surface from "feature suite" to "operator."

3. **EU AI Act + GDPR maturity created a regulatory moat for EU-anchored builders.** US-rooted incumbents will struggle to comply at depth; an EU-anchored, EU-resident, audit-log-as-spine product earns trust the incumbents can only retrofit.

## Why us

- We've watched 100,000 Tuesdays — figuratively. Founders' family lineage in Irish service trades; tour of European salons in 2024–2025; ~50 Tuesday-walk shadow sessions with owners across IE/UK/DE/FR/ES.
- We chose the wedge cell (P2b solo Hair English-IE) because the founder *is* in adjacency to that customer; we know the texture of Conor's Sunday-evening Excel session because we sat through them.
- We hired the eval lead before we hired the second engineer. We bought into ADRs before we shipped features. We chose the slow path because it's the right path.
- We refused the chatbot bubble before we built the agent. Brand discipline precedes feature discipline.

## Why Ireland

- **Closest customer.** Dublin is dense with single-shop owners who feel Phorest's incumbency tax most acutely.
- **Anglophone EU.** Liv ships in English-IE first; localisation expands from there.
- **Talent pool.** Dublin tech + a European employment posture lets us hire across EU without visa friction.
- **Time-zone bridge.** CET-aligned for EU customers; reasonable overlap for UK; clean separation from US incumbent rhythms.
- **Story-fit.** Ireland's service-trade culture (the chair-rental barbershop; the family salon over three generations) is the canonical Livia customer.

## The 5-year vision

By end of year 5: **operator-as-a-service for European appointment-based service businesses.**

- **Geography:** IE + UK + Nordics + DACH + France + Iberia (per `geographic-expansion.md`).
- **Verticals:** Hair, Beauty, Body art, Wellness, Fitness as primary; Medspa + Allied health emerging.
- **Customers:** ≥10,000 paying tenants. ≥150,000 staff using Liv weekly. ≥3M customers booked through Livia.
- **Category position:** named, defended, taught. New entrants describe themselves in our terms ("AI-native operator for…").
- **Org:** ≥80 people. Headquartered in Dublin; hubs in 2–3 EU cities. Cash-positive or strategically funded.

## The 20-year vision

**The trust-amplification layer for any hierarchical service business.**

If Livia works for European appointment-based services, the same architectural commitments — per-character agent depth, audit log as product surface, scoped role hierarchy, EU-anchored regulatory posture — generalise to:

- Independent practitioners with delegation needs (legal, accounting, dental, veterinary).
- Hospitality hierarchies (hotels, restaurants).
- Field-service businesses (trades, home services, mobile health).
- Education hierarchies (tutoring, private schools, vocational programmes).

The 20-year company is **Liv inside the operating fabric of European service work** — wherever a human delegates to another human under hierarchy and trust, Liv's architecture has a place.

We don't build for that 20-year vision yet. We build for the 5-year version. The 20-year version is the gravitational pull that informs which architectural choices we make today (the audit log + the per-character runtime + the cross-tenant intelligence privacy posture all earn the right to graduate to those 20-year markets later).
