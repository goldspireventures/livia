# Exit and acquirer thesis — F10

**Status:** F10 (2026-05-07). Most speculative doc in the foundation. Founder + closest advisors. Revisited annually.

## Posture

We build the company so an exit is **an option, not the only path.** A great company has multiple credible paths: scale-and-IPO, strategic acquisition, founder-led independence, private-equity recapitalisation. We optimise for optionality.

We do **not** build to be acquired. Companies built to be acquired tend to under-invest in the durable architecture that makes them genuinely valuable to acquirers. We build the durable architecture; if an acquirer wants it, the option exists.

## Strategic acquirers in 5–7 years

### Phorest

- **Probability of approach:** Medium-high.
- **Fit:** Vertical consolidation. Phorest's chain depth + Livia's AI-native + multi-brand + chair-rental.
- **Estimated price range:** €120–250M depending on revenue + growth at the time.
- **What we'd build to maximise the scenario:** Continue aggressive IE/UK/Nordics expansion (their core geographies); deepen the chain-rollup reporting (their moat we eat into); deliver the audit-log + per-character agent at production scale (the things they can't replicate quickly).
- **Cultural fit risk:** Medium — Phorest is Dublin-anchored, B2B-software-mature; Liv's brand voice differs but is compatible.

### Treatwell

- **Probability of approach:** Medium.
- **Fit:** Vertical depth + customer-ownership trade. Treatwell wants depth into salon ops to defend marketplace cut.
- **Estimated price range:** €100–200M.
- **What we'd build:** Continued non-marketplace posture is value-creating for Treatwell (it's a moat against Fresha); we don't change strategy.
- **Cultural fit risk:** Medium-high — Treatwell is marketplace-first; we're operator-first; ideological fit is uncertain.

### Toast

- **Probability of approach:** Low-medium.
- **Fit:** Cross-vertical AI play. Toast (restaurants) → salons (Livia) → other appointment-based (acquired or built).
- **Estimated price range:** €150–300M (Toast is well-capitalised; willing to pay premium for category-defining acquisitions).
- **What we'd build:** The "operator-as-a-service" frame generalises beyond salons; that's intrinsically valuable to a Toast.
- **Cultural fit risk:** Medium — US-rooted; we'd need EU-autonomy guarantees.

### Square (Block)

- **Probability of approach:** Low-medium.
- **Fit:** Payments + AI vertical. Square already in salon-adjacent (Square Appointments).
- **Estimated price range:** €120–250M.
- **What we'd build:** Don't bend the payments stack toward Square's; if they want us, they take us as we are.
- **Cultural fit risk:** Medium — US-rooted; payments-first vs operator-first frame difference.

### Booksy (or whoever owns Booksy by year 5)

- **Probability of approach:** Low.
- **Fit:** Geographic complement (Booksy strong US/PL; we strong EU).
- **Estimated price range:** €80–180M.
- **What we'd build:** No special accommodation.

### Private equity rollup

- **Probability of approach:** High over years 4–7.
- **Fit:** Multi-vertical SaaS rollup thesis. PE firms accumulate vertical SaaS plays.
- **Estimated price range:** Variable, depending on EBITDA.
- **What we'd build:** Cash-positive operations earlier than a venture-only path would. The "cash-positive at scale" optionality.
- **Cultural risk:** High — PE rollups often hollow out culture; this is the path we'd accept only at meaningful premium.

### "Frontier-AI lab" acquirer (OpenAI, Anthropic, similar EU lab)

- **Probability of approach:** Low-medium.
- **Fit:** Vertical AI deployment proof + EU regulatory expertise + agent-runtime research value.
- **Estimated price range:** Hard to bound — could be premium for the eval-framework + regulatory posture as much as the salon business.
- **What we'd build:** Continue deepening eval framework + EU AI Act compliance posture; these are valuable beyond salons.

## What this implies for what we build

- **Don't duplicate what likely acquirers already do well.** Phorest's chain rollup, Treatwell's marketplace, Toast's payments stack, Square's payment infra. We build alongside, not against.
- **Build what they'd pay to acquire.** The per-character agent, the audit-log-as-spine, the cross-tenant differential-privacy intelligence, the EU AI Act posture at depth, the brand-of-Liv. These are the things no acquirer can replicate quickly.
- **Cash discipline matters even on venture-funded path.** A path-to-cash-positive at customer 5,000 makes us acquisition-attractive AND independent-viable.

## The IPO scenario — honest assessment

- **Probability:** Low in 5 years; medium in 7–10.
- **Triggers:** ≥€100M ARR; multi-market presence; defensible category leadership; favourable EU IPO market.
- **Honest read:** EU SaaS IPOs are rare and challenging; AI-tagged narratives may help in 2028–2030 windows; we don't optimise for this path but we don't foreclose it.

## The independent path

- **Probability:** Medium-high.
- **Why we'd choose it:** The 20-year vision (`founding-story.md`) is bigger than any acquirer's current strategic frame. The trust-amplification layer for European service work generally is a multi-decade build that benefits from independent control.
- **Constraints:** Requires cash-positive operations at scale; requires founder + leadership team appetite for the long game; requires investor patience or capital-restructuring to PE.

## What we tell investors about exit

> Our preferred outcome is to build Livia into the operator-as-a-service category leader for European service businesses, and to remain independent through year 7+ to realise the broader trust-amplification thesis. Strategic acquirers exist (Phorest, Treatwell, Toast, Square, PE rollups) and we expect approaches; we evaluate them at the time. We optimise for optionality, not for any specific exit path.
