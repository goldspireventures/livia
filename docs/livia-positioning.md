## Section 1 — The depth commitment table

The *populated cells* (persona × configuration) we serve, with Liv's ambition rung at four temporal checkpoints, plus the support layer behind her.

**Rung legend:**

- **R1 — Polite Secretary.** Reads, suggests, asks. Acts only on explicit confirmation.
- **R2 — Active Concierge.** Acts on small, reversible decisions inside policy. Asks before anything irreversible.
- **R3 — Floor Manager.** Acts on most operational decisions inside policy. Escalates the genuinely judgement-laden.
- **R4 — Fractional COO.** Drives outcomes (revenue, retention, schedule integrity). Negotiates on the salon's behalf.
- **R5 — Operator.** Runs the salon end-to-end inside the owner's stated values; reports, doesn't ask.

**Support layer legend:** None / In-app help / Concierge (human ops backstop) / 24-7 ops.

| Cell — persona × configuration | Launch | +6 mo | +12 mo | +24 mo | Support layer |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **P1 Founder** × multi-shop small chain (2-5) | R2 | R3 | R4 | R5 | Concierge from R3 |
| **P1 Founder** × multi-brand portfolio | R1 | R2 | R3 | R4 | Concierge from R2 |
| **P2a Owner-with-Mgr** × single-shop mature | R2 | R3 | R4 | R5 | In-app → concierge from R4 |
| **P2b Owner-no-Mgr** × single-shop owner+staff | R2 | R3 | R3 | R4 | In-app help |
| **P2b Owner-no-Mgr** × solo-shop (the working owner) | R3 | R4 | R4 | R5 | None — *Liv is the team* |
| **P3 Manager** × single-shop with mgr | R1 | R2 | R3 | R4 | In-app help |
| **P3 Manager** × multi-shop chain (per-shop) | R1 | R2 | R3 | R4 | In-app help |
| **P4a Senior STAFF** × single-shop with mgr | R1 | R1 | R2 | R2 | In-app — **bounded by client trust** |
| **P4a Senior STAFF** × chair-rental (dual identity) | R2 | R3 | R3 | R4 | In-app — *Liv runs her micro-business* |
| **P4b Senior-w-admin** × single-shop mature | R1 | R2 | R3 | R3 | In-app help |
| **P5 Junior STAFF** × single-shop with mgr | R1 | R1 | R2 | R2 | In-app help |
| **P6 Receptionist** × single-shop with mgr | R2 | R3 | R3 | R4 | In-app help |
| **P6 Receptionist** × multi-shop chain | R2 | R3 | R4 | R4 | In-app help |
| **P7 Customer** (universal across configurations) | R2 | R3 | R3 | R4 | None |
| **P7 Customer** × no-app variant (WhatsApp/SMS/voice only) | R2 | R3 | R3 | R4 | None |

**Why this asymmetry — the persona-asymmetric depth discipline:**

- **Owners get the deepest depth.** The operator wedge lives here. We commit to R5 in 24 months for solo and multi-shop owners.
- **Solo owners get *more* than mid-size owners at launch.** When there is no team to delegate to, Liv *is* the team. R3 from day one for the working owner.
- **Managers get genuine capability, but always answering up.** Maxes at R4. Liv proposes; the Owner ratifies on the hard things.
- **Staff in the *bounded-by-client-trust* role max at R2.** This is the discipline: for P4a Senior STAFF on a host shop's payroll and P5 Junior STAFF, Liv assists, never intermediates. Going deeper would betray the very humans we depend on. The Senior's relationship with her client is sacred.
- **Two named exceptions exist** and the table reflects them. (a) **Chair-rental Seniors** (P4a × chair-rental) are effectively founders of their own micro-business inside the host shop — we give them owner-grade depth (R4) for *their* book, against *their* customers, never the host's. (b) **Senior-with-admin** (P4b) is a hybrid role with explicit ADMIN authority granted by the Owner — depth tracks the granted authority (R3), not the staff floor. Anywhere else, the R2 ceiling is hard.
- **Customers get deep-but-soft depth.** R4 in 24 months. Liv talks to customers like a great concierge — efficient, warm, never pushy. The depth is in capability, not aggressiveness.
- **The no-app customer matters as much as the app customer.** Same rung, different surface.

**Verticals overlay:** Launch verticals are Hair + Beauty (heartland). Wellness, Body art, and Fitness are +12 mo. Medspa and Allied health are +24 mo with regulatory partnerships. Dental and Mental health are explicitly never (without specialised partners).

## Section 3 — The category we choose, derived

**Livia is the colleague your salon hires. Her name is Liv.**

Not "salon software." Not "AI-native salon platform." Not even "operating system." Liv is a *named colleague* who happens to be software. Owners hire her, promote her, depend on her, brag about her, and would feel her loss if she left. The product surface (dashboard, mobile app, voice line, WhatsApp) is the *means* by which the colleague is reachable, not the thing itself.

**Defended sentence-by-sentence against the prior asserted line** ("premium AI-native multi-tenant OS for EU/Ireland appointment-based service businesses"):

- **"Premium"** — survives. Aurora-Midnight aesthetic, Cormorant Garamond serif. Premium is the texture, not the positioning.
- **"AI-native"** — too narrow. Every product calls itself AI-native in 2026. The phrase no longer differentiates.
- **"Multi-tenant OS"** — backend description, not positioning. Belongs in the engineering README.
- **"EU/Ireland appointment-based service businesses"** — describes the WHO, not the WHAT. Belongs in the GTM doc.

The new line names the WHAT in the smallest number of words: a colleague, with a name. Everything else (verticals, configurations, modalities, EU residency, the trust ratchet, the audit-as-diary) is a *proof point* of this line — not the line itself.

## Section 4 — Wedge map

Six unfair advantages, ranked by durability. Each: why defensible, who would have to copy to neutralise, cost of copying, time-to-copy.

### W1 — Liv-as-colleague (the unit primitive itself) — *most durable*

- **Why defensible:** the unit primitive shift from "tool" to "colleague" requires reorganising the company around a character, not a feature surface. Every downstream design choice (audit-as-diary, trust ratchet, "Liv was wrong" surface, voice with character) inherits this.
- **Who would have to copy:** Phorest, Fresha, Square, Mindbody — anyone in the category.
- **Cost to copy:** rebuild company culture + product team + hire character writers + accept the discipline of saying *no* to features that don't fit a colleague.
- **Time-to-copy:** 18-36 months minimum. Most won't try because the identity shift is too disruptive to their existing customer base.

### W2 — Voice modality with character — *very durable*

- **Why defensible:** Liv answers the phone with the same voice and personality that signs the audit log and writes the digest. Latency, character, and per-voice memory are co-designed.
- **Who would have to copy:** any competitor who wants to compete on the deepest revenue-leak surface (busy lines lose bookings).
- **Cost to copy:** voice stack + character/voice directors + latency-vs-character tradeoffs + per-tenant voice memory.
- **Time-to-copy:** 12-18 months for engineering + 12+ months for character work. Phorest is unlikely to invest because their architecture isn't AI-native.

### W3 — Configuration-and-vertical native simultaneously — *durable, compounding*

- **Why defensible:** the only product whose data model and workflows natively understand chair-rental, franchise, multi-brand, partnership, AND the vertical-specific rituals (medspa consent, tattoo design proof, physio progress notes, fitness class capacity) without forcing them into one canonical shape.
- **Who would have to copy:** Mindbody (single-vertical-deep, multi-config-shallow), Phorest (single-config-deep, single-vertical-deep), Fresha (marketplace shape).
- **Cost to copy:** refactor data model + hire vertical specialists per category + accept multi-config product complexity.
- **Time-to-copy:** 24+ months for any competitor with a single-shape data model. Compounds because every cell we serve well grows our defensibility — the under-served owners (chair-rental barbershop chains, multi-brand portfolios) come to us by *exclusion* from elsewhere.

### W4 — EU-anchored sovereignty as worldview — *durable, growing with regulation*

- **Why defensible:** Irish cultural surface (Liv speaks Gaeilge symbolically), EU data residency, AI-Act-compliant by design with auditable AI reasoning, customer-protection-aware, employment-law-aware. Posture as moat.
- **Who would have to copy:** US-led incumbents (Square, Mindbody, Booksy) cannot match without restructuring.
- **Cost to copy:** relocate, restructure data, retrain teams on EU regulatory thinking.
- **Time-to-copy:** effectively never for the US incumbents. UK-headquartered (Phorest, Treatwell) could copy but lose their cross-Atlantic marketing.

### W5 — Trust ratchet + audit-as-diary + "Liv was wrong" surface — *medium-durable, brand-anchored*

- **Why defensible:** the trust ratchet is a designed product primitive (promotion, not configuration). The audit log as Liv's diary is a tone-and-format choice that competitors with feature parity will still build as a CSV table. "Liv was wrong" is a designed apology surface, not a fallback.
- **Who would have to copy:** any AI-native competitor.
- **Cost to copy:** design + ML + change management + the discipline to refuse the "settings panel" pattern.
- **Time-to-copy:** 12 months. Defensible through *first-mover branding* — once owners associate the trust ratchet with Liv, copies feel derivative.

### W6 — Hotel-principle persona depth + persona-asymmetric discipline — *medium-durable, organisationally hard*

- **Why defensible:** the discipline to refuse "OWNER screen with bits hidden" for STAFF and to *bound* Liv's depth at the human relationship layer is rare. It requires organisational stance, not just engineering. Refusing to give the Senior Stylist's clients to Liv is a strategic choice most competitors would never make.
- **Who would have to copy:** anyone who claims to be persona-aware.
- **Cost to copy:** an organisational stance against feature-creep, plus per-persona surface investment.
- **Time-to-copy:** hard to copy at all. Most competitors will *say* they do it and ship "OWNER screen with rows hidden" anyway.

## Section 5 — What we are explicitly NOT

Each "not" is tied to a depth-map decision and to a competitor we deliberately disqualify.

- **NOT a marketplace.** (Fresha, Treatwell.) We don't take a cut of bookings. We don't aggregate demand. We don't compete with the salon for the customer. Each salon owns its customer relationship absolutely. *Tied to:* customer-typology design — the customer belongs to the salon, not the platform. **Reconciliation with Bet 6 (Liv Mark + `liv.io/find`):** the discovery surface is a *quality registry*, not a marketplace. It (a) takes no commission on bookings made via it, (b) does not collect or hold customer payment, (c) sends customers to the salon's own booking surface (the salon owns the conversion), and (d) is salon-opt-in only. If we ever cannot defend those four lines simultaneously, we delete the registry. The line is hard.
- **NOT payments-led.** (Square Appointments.) Payments are infrastructure, not the product. Liv is the product. We accept payments because Liv handles deposits and refunds; not because we're a payments company in disguise.
- **NOT enterprise medspa.** (Zenoti, Pabau.) Medspa is +24mo with regulatory partnerships; we won't bend the product to enterprise medical workflows that would dilute the heartland (hair + beauty).
- **NOT a generic appointment tool.** (Acuity, Calendly.) Generic horizontal scheduling is a different product. Livia is *people-business specific* with vertical and configuration depth.
- **NOT a CRM with appointments bolted on.** (HubSpot, Pipedrive.) The org primitive is the salon team and its hierarchy, not the lead funnel.
- **NOT an AI bolt-on inside an existing tool.** (Phorest+AI, Fresha+AI.) Liv is the unit primitive. The dashboard is her means; she is not a chatbot in the dashboard.
- **NOT a chain-management tool for franchises.** (Boulevard.) Franchise is a configuration we serve; not the category we lead with.
- **NOT a self-employed-stylist app.** (GlossGenius, Boulevard solo.) Solo is a configuration we serve; not the category we lead with.
- **NOT a clinical EHR.** (Cliniko, Jane.) Allied health is +24mo lite; we will not become a regulated clinical record system. Where customers need clinical depth, we partner.
- **NOT a tipping app.** (Tippy.) Tips are a feature, not a category.

The discipline of *what we are not* protects the integrity of the unit primitive. Every "yes" to one of the above would dilute "the colleague your salon hires" into a Swiss-army-knife product nobody would name.

## Section 6 — The public category line

**Livia is the operating system for people-businesses in Europe. Liv is the colleague you hire — and she earns more responsibility over time.**

*Subhead:* "She answers the phone, runs your bookings, knows your regulars, and keeps the day's chaos in one calm place — on web, mobile, and the channels you already use."

**Deprecated (GTM residue — do not use in product copy):** "the colleague your **salon** hires."

**Defended:**

- **"Colleague"** — establishes the unit primitive immediately. Not "tool," not "software," not "platform."
- **"Your salon hires"** — establishes the buyer (the salon as an entity, not just the owner) and the relationship (employment, not subscription).
- **"Her name is Liv"** — names the character. Makes her real.
- **"Answers the phone"** — leads with the most concrete, least faked claim. The deepest single revenue-leak surface in the industry.
- **"Earns more responsibility every month"** — the trust ratchet, in plain words.
- **"Until she's running the place"** — the ambition, stated honestly.

This is the line. Everything else on `livia.io` is a proof point of this line — not the line itself.

The previous asserted line ("premium AI-native multi-tenant OS for EU/Ireland appointment-based service businesses") is reserved for the engineering README and the data-room one-pager, not the marketing surface.
