# Messaging by persona

**Status:** v1 (2026-05-07). Reads with `docs/personas.md`, `docs/brand/voice.md`.

What Livia says to each persona — across landing pages, in-product copy, sales conversations, support replies. Per-persona registers; per-persona pain anchors; per-persona "yes" moment.

## The structure per persona

Each persona below has:
- **The pain anchor** — what they feel in language.
- **What we say (5-second pitch)** — the one sentence.
- **What we say (30-second pitch)** — the elevator.
- **What we don't say** — the language traps.
- **Yes moment** — the specific phrase that lands.

---

## P1 Founder (multi-shop chain owner — Aoife pattern)

### Pain anchor
"I have three shops. Two are doing well. The Galway one is leaking customers and I don't know why and I don't have time to figure it out."

### 5-second pitch
"Liv runs your shops; your weekly briefing tells you which one needs you on Sunday."

### 30-second pitch
"You have shops; you don't have time to be in all of them. Liv handles the day-to-day at each — bookings, follow-ups, the staff awkward stuff — and at the end of the week tells you which shop needs your attention. Not a dashboard you have to read; a paragraph from someone who watched all three."

### What we don't say
- "Multi-location dashboard" (sounds like Salesforce).
- "Roll-up reports" (corporate; cold).
- "Manage your portfolio" (you're a shop owner, not a hedge fund).

### Yes moment
> "Sunday I'll know which shop needs me, without reading three reports."

## P2a Owner-with-Mgr (single-shop with Manager — Roisín pattern)

### Pain anchor
"My Manager is great but she's drowning. Half the things she does don't actually need her — they need a system. The other half nobody else can do."

### 5-second pitch
"Liv handles the half your Manager doesn't have to. So she can do the half only she can."

### 30-second pitch
"Your Manager is doing two jobs: running the shop and putting out fires that shouldn't be fires. Liv takes the second job — the rebookings, the tone-perfect customer DMs, the 'we don't take cash but we forgot to update the menu' — so your Manager can keep doing the first job. You see it in the briefing: less Manager-firefighting, more Manager-leading."

### What we don't say
- "Empower your Manager" (precious).
- "Free up Manager time" (passive; jargony).
- "Streamline operations" (don't.).

### Yes moment
> "She'll have her Saturdays back."

## P2b Owner-no-Mgr (single-shop without Manager — Conor pattern; the wedge)

### Pain anchor
"It's just me. I cut hair eight hours; I do everything else in the gaps. The DMs alone eat my lunch every day."

### 5-second pitch
"Liv runs the operations side; you keep cutting hair."

### 30-second pitch
"You're running a one-person business that needs three people. Liv is the second person — answers the DMs, manages your bookings, handles the awkward 'can I move my appointment again' chains, takes the call when you're with a client. Not a virtual receptionist; an operator. By month two, you'll wonder how you did it without."

### What we don't say
- "AI receptionist" (just "Liv").
- "Booking software" (we're more than that).
- "Save time" (he knows what he saves; specific).

### Yes moment
> "Lunch back. Voicemail empty. Tuesday-morning panic: gone."

## P3 Manager (Niamh pattern)

### Pain anchor
"My Owner thinks I do less than I do. The Liv-handles-it stuff used to be a third of my day."

### 5-second pitch
"Liv covers the firefight; the briefing makes your work visible."

### 30-second pitch
"You know how much of your day is fighting fires that shouldn't be fires. Liv does that. AND — when Liv handles something, it's logged in the audit log AND mentioned in the Sunday briefing your Owner reads. Your job becomes more visible, not less. You become the person doing the things only you can do."

### What we don't say
- "Free up Manager bandwidth" (corporate).
- "Make Owner happy" (assumes a problem).
- "Manager dashboard" (just "your view").

### Yes moment
> "He'll see what I actually do."

## P4 Senior-w-admin (Sarah pattern; the role no incumbent has)

### Pain anchor
"I do half the Manager's job but I'm not paid for it and Owner can never find me when he wants to delegate."

### 5-second pitch
"Liv gives you scope — within a cap, you can act. The audit shows it."

### 30-second pitch
"Most software has Manager or Staff. You're between. Liv (and Livia) treat that as a real role — you have scoped authority, you can issue refunds up to a cap, you can approve time-off in your team. Audit log shows your work. Owner sees it in the Sunday briefing. The Manager-track promotion that's been informal becomes formal, and visible."

### What we don't say
- "Permissions" (too IT).
- "Power user" (gamer).
- "Admin role" (cold).

### Yes moment
> "I get the scope I deserve, and Owner sees it."

## P5 Staff (Lara pattern)

### Pain anchor
"My day starts late because nobody told me my 9am was a no-show."

### 5-second pitch
"My Day. Customer prefs. Push when something matters. The rest, Liv handles."

### 30-second pitch
"You see your day on your phone. Your customers' preferences are there. If something changes, you get a notification — once, no spam. The DMs, the rebooking dance, the 'can you fit me in' begging — Liv handles those. Time-off you can submit straight from your phone; routine ones get approved without you chasing your Manager."

### What we don't say
- "Empower yourself" (gross).
- "Take control of your schedule" (too American).
- "Workflow management" (god no).

### Yes moment
> "I open my phone. I know my day. The customer's name is right."

## P6 Receptionist (Síobhan pattern)

### Pain anchor
"I'm at the desk and the phone is ringing and there's a queue and I'm trying to do three things at once."

### 5-second pitch
"Liv answers the phone; you handle the desk."

### 30-second pitch
"The phone-vs-desk juggle is the worst part of your job. Liv answers the calls — books real appointments, takes messages, knows when to push to you. You handle the people standing in front of you. The day at the desk gets calmer, not noisier."

### What we don't say
- "Front-desk automation" (cold).
- "Reduce phone burden" (corporate).
- "Multi-task" (we want you single-tasked).

### Yes moment
> "The phone stops being the enemy."

## P7 Customer (Mary pattern; CT2 Regular and CT3 New)

### Pain anchor
"I want to book. I don't want to download anything. I don't want to call. I want to message and have it sorted."

### 5-second pitch
"Message the business. Liv (their AI colleague) will sort you out."

### 30-second pitch
"Your shop uses Liv to handle bookings + reschedules. You message them — WhatsApp, SMS, however you usually do — and Liv replies fast, books you in, remembers what you usually get. If you need a human, Liv puts you through. Your data stays with the business, not anywhere else."

### What we don't say
- "AI booking experience" (cold).
- "Conversational interface" (jargon).
- "Self-service portal" (god no).

### Yes moment
> "I message; I'm booked. No app. No phone tag."

## Cross-cutting

### What Livia says to "the press" / industry observers

> Livia is the operating service for appointment-based businesses in the EU. We're not a chatbot bolted onto scheduling software. We're an agent that runs the day-to-day so the operator can do the work only they can.

### What Livia says to investors

> Operator-as-a-service. EU-anchored. Trust-amplification posture. The wedge is Hair-vertical English-IE single-shop; the heartland is + chair-rental + small chain. We measure depth-per-cell, not user count.

### What Livia says when something breaks

> We got <X> wrong. Here's what happened: <specific>. Here's what we're doing: <specific>. Refund / credit / fix coming by <specific date>.

### What Livia never says

- "We apologise for any inconvenience."
- "Your call is important to us."
- "We value your feedback." (We do; we don't say that.)
- "We're committed to..." (commitment is shown, not announced.)
- "We're disrupting the industry."

## Per-channel register

| Channel | Register | Length cap |
|---|---|---|
| Marketing site headline | Bold; declarative; serif | ≤14 words |
| Marketing site body | Calm; specific; sans | ≤22 words / sentence |
| In-product owner cockpit | Specific; short | ≤14 words / sentence |
| In-product mobile briefing | Specific; warm | ≤14 words / sentence |
| Customer-facing DM (Liv) | Warm; short; AI-disclosed | ≤18 words / sentence |
| Voice (Liv) | Calm; deliberate; AI-disclosed | Natural pacing |
| Sales email | First-person founder; specific | ≤4 paragraphs |
| Support reply | First-person CS; specific outcome | ≤3 paragraphs |
| Status page incident | Honest; specific surface; specific time | ≤3 sentences |
| Changelog entry | Specific; customer-language | ≤2 sentences / row |
