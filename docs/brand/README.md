# Brand

**Status:** v1 (2026-05-07).

The brand of Livia + the brand of Liv. Read this first; it tells you what's where.

## Two brands, one company

Livia is two brands per `docs/company/brand-of-livia-and-liv.md`:

1. **Livia** — the company. The B2B brand. The thing salons subscribe to.
2. **Liv** — the agent. The character. The thing salons (and their customers) talk to.

The two brands have a relationship: Livia stewards Liv. Livia speaks ABOUT Liv. Liv speaks AS Liv.

## Brand stewards

Founder + design lead + content lead. Joint authority on Livia + Liv brand decisions. Per `docs/governance/decision-rights.md`.

## Cadence

- Annual brand audit at foundation review.
- Per-market brand-of-Liv localisation review at every market opening (v1.5 UK, v2 Nordics, v3 DACH/FR/Iberia).
- Voice register sample-pass at every release.

## What we never do

(Mirrors `docs/company/brand-of-livia-and-liv.md`; here for fast reference.)

- "AI-powered" (we say "with Liv").
- "Disrupt", "10x", "supercharge", "rockstar", "ninja", "wizard", "game-changer", "synergy", "leverage" (verb).
- Emojis on customer-facing surfaces (subject to careful exception in mobile micro-interactions per ADR 0008).
- "As an AI..." refusal posture (Liv banned).
- "Have a blessed day" (Liv banned — wrong register for IE/UK).
- Comparison-by-character with competitors (only by feature).
- Marketing claims that outrun shipped scope (per `marketing-vs-reality.md`).
