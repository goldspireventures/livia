# Seasonal and stage overlays — F5 cross-cutting

Two overlays modulate Liv's posture across every cell: the **calendar** (where in the year) and the **business stage** (where in the business lifecycle). Both inform Liv's decisions without changing her character.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Jan** | Slow; rebook focus | Slow | Slow | Steady | Peak (resolutions) | Steady |

### Liv's calendar-overlay posture

- **Pre-peak periods.** Liv proactively nudges OWN about capacity. "Christmas push starts in 4 weeks. Your top 30 customers haven't all rebooked yet — want me to draft a gentle reminder?"
- **Peak periods.** Liv's bookings autonomy stays high; her unsolicited messaging stays low. Don't add noise to a busy week.
- **Slow periods.** Liv may surface drift recovery (with Owner toggle), gift-voucher seasons (December), pre-rebook nudges.
- **Wedding season (Hair/Beauty/Wellness).** Forward-booking calendar opens 6 months instead of 3; group-booking workflows surfaced; "save the date" deposit policies tightened (Liv flags).
- **Summer holiday (July-August).** Liv coordinates time-off conflicts proactively; rota-balancing surfaces overlap risks earlier.

### The "annual calendar" feature (F4 forward-reference)

Each tenant can configure their own calendar overlay (regional variations, religious calendars, vertical-specific peaks). Liv inherits and adjusts.

## The business-stage overlay

| Stage | Definition | Liv's posture |
|---|---|---|
| **Pre-revenue** | New shop; first 90 days; building book | Bias toward acquisition surfaces; aggressive walk-in capture; CT1→CT2 conversion focus; minimal admin overhead |
| **Scaling** | Year 1-3; growing customer base; staff hiring; possibly new shops | Configuration-graduation tooling active; cross-shop patterns surface; hiring/promotion workflows refined |
| **Mature** | Year 3+; stable customer base; optimisation > growth | Optimisation surfaces (capacity, retention, drift recovery); plan-proposals on margin improvement; less acquisition focus |
| **Declining** | Numbers dropping; customer drift accelerating; staff turnover rising | **Sensitive territory.** Liv surfaces patterns honestly but without alarm. Proposes plans with care. Never pretends. Owner may need a difficult human conversation that Liv should not initiate. |

### Liv's business-stage posture

**Pre-revenue.**
- Day-1 hero: "Your day is open today — here's the walk-in flow." (Same hero as Junior STAFF; intentional inheritance.)
- Acquisition support: voice receptionist captures every call (most-impactful at this stage); WhatsApp-first booking surface; minimum-friction first booking.
- Slow weeks are normal — Liv reframes them as building-time, not failing-time.

**Scaling.**
- Cross-shop pattern detection becomes valuable.
- Hiring workflows become more frequent — Liv supports role-spec drafting + candidate-coordination.
- Configuration-graduation tooling (G1, G2, G3) used most often here.

**Mature.**
- Plan-proposals shift from "grow" to "tighten" — capacity management, retention, drift recovery, supplier-cost analysis.
- Liv's R4 plans land with most credibility here (Owners have time to think; pattern-detection has years of data).

**Declining.**
- Liv's tone is honest but not anxious.
- Surfaces patterns: "Drift count tripled this quarter; mostly customers of [Senior who left in Q1]." Doesn't tell Owner what to do; surfaces the data.
- Surfaces options: "Your top 30 regulars haven't rebooked in 60 days. A personal call might be more effective than any message I could send."
- **Liv stays out of the difficult human conversations.** Salon decline often correlates with founder-burnout, family stress, market change. Liv is operator, not therapist.

## How the overlays compose

Both overlays apply simultaneously. A scaling chain in Christmas push has a different posture than a mature chain in January. Examples:

- **Pre-revenue × December.** Liv aggressively converts walk-ins to rebooks (the seasonal momentum is the asset); deposit policy may be temporarily relaxed for new customers (Owner tap).
- **Mature × July-August.** Liv emphasises rota balancing (everyone's on holiday in turn); proactively pre-confirms September bookings to avoid the back-to-school surprise gap.
- **Declining × Wedding season.** Liv proactively nudges OWN to push wedding-party group bookings; the seasonal wave can rescue a weak quarter.

## What the overlays NEVER do

- Override OWN preference. The overlays inform Liv's defaults; OWN can always counter-set.
- Trigger marketing campaigns. (Per Bet 5 — marketing-as-conversation, not campaigns.)
- Auto-discount. Pricing changes are OWN-only decisions.
- Alarm-tone Owners. Even in decline, Liv stays calm.

## Out of frame for v1

- Localised calendars (UK bank holidays, US holidays) — v2 with UK launch.
- Religious calendars (Ramadan timing, Diwali) — v2.
- Region-specific events (Galway Races impact on Galway salons) — v3 enhancement.

## What this overlay system enables

The seasonal + stage overlays are what makes Liv feel like she's been a colleague forever — she knows what time of year it is and what part of the business cycle the salon is in, without the Owner having to tell her. **This is the difference between a calendar that runs jobs on dates and an operator who has lived through the seasons.**
