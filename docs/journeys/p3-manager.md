# Journey — P3 Manager × Single-shop with mgr × Hair

**The cell.** Niamh Byrne. Manages Aurora Studio's floor for Roisín. 14 staff. Has been running the place for 3 years; Roisín hands off most operations.

**Why a separate journey from P2a Owner.** The Manager's relationship with Liv is fundamentally different — Liv reports to her on staff matters; Liv asks her for ratifications; Liv defers to her on the floor. Owner-side journeys often miss the Manager's daily texture.

## First touch

- Niamh hears about Liv from a Receptionist peer.
- Shows it to Roisín; they decide together.
- Niamh's posture is cautious — "I've seen software promise things and then fail to handle the actual day."

## Sign-up & onboarding

- Niamh is on the founder-led onboarding call. She asks the hardest questions ("what happens when Lara doesn't show? what happens when a customer Wants Me Specifically?").
- Each gets a concrete answer with workflow-walkthrough.
- Niamh helps set the cap ladder; promotes Sarah to ADM-D with her endorsement.

## First day

- Reads the morning briefing on phone over coffee. *"3 voicemails from yesterday's after-hours, all answered by me overnight (you'll find them on your queue). Today: 32 booked, Lara solid week, Sarah's first scoped approval pending."*
- Stand-up at 8:30 — references Liv's overnight handle as if she's another colleague.
- First voice call routed to her queue at 9:14 (a customer wanted to talk to a "real person" — Liv's hand-off behaviour).

## First week

- Niamh begins to relax. The phone really doesn't ring at the desk anymore — only when a customer specifically asks for her.
- First scoped time-off via Sarah works cleanly. Niamh notices: she didn't have to be involved in 3/4 of the workflow.
- First above-cap refund routes to Roisín; Roisín approves; Niamh sees the resolution surface in her queue. The cap-ladder removes a friction point that used to take 30-90 min of her day.
- First Friday digest to Niamh (Manager-scoped, not Owner-scoped): the week, the patterns at her level, the one thing Roisín might want to discuss Thursday.

## First month

- R3 reached for Niamh. Liv handles routine refunds within her cap autonomously (with audit-log entry); Niamh sees a daily summary.
- Re-rota when Lara calls in sick (week 3) is the demonstrative moment. Liv's rebook drafts arrive 15 min after the call-in; Niamh edits one, sends three as-is. Time saved: ~2 hours.
- First "Liv was wrong" moment as Manager: Liv approved a refund within cap that Niamh would have escalated (she knew the customer was a regular complainer). Niamh flags; Liv adds the customer-context note (CT4 already classified — Niamh hadn't realised Liv knew); Liv apologises, refunds the salon's loss to ledger, and the next time will escalate.

## First quarter

- Niamh's day looks different. Her day-start review takes 15 min (was 45). Her phone stops ringing through cuts (it really does — voice line takes most of it). Her late-evening DM coverage drops (Liv handles).
- She gets her first 1-on-1-prep digest (Liv summarises a STA's quarter for the upcoming review).
- She trusts the cap-ladder enough to start raising her own cap (asks Roisín to bump it from €100 to €150).
- She becomes a Liv advocate to her peers at other salons.

## First year

- She covers Roisín's holiday (10 days) — elevated authority works smoothly.
- Year-end review with Roisín references Liv's audit log for several decisions. Niamh feels seen and trusted at a level she didn't before.
- Personal development: she's now able to think about the salon strategically (vs operationally), which Roisín had wanted for years but Niamh had never had time for.

## Lifecycle moments designed for

- **First Manager-side digest (week 1, Friday).** Different from Owner's — pattern + her workload, not the P&L.
- **First scoped time-off via ADM-D (week 1).** She sees her load lighten visibly.
- **First above-cap refund (within first 2 weeks).** Removes a friction point.
- **First "Liv was wrong" as Manager (month 1).** The refinement of Liv's classifier from Niamh's tacit knowledge.
- **First holiday cover (month 9-10).** The trust-up moment — she runs the chain with elevated authority; Liv runs the operational tier.
- **First peer recommendation she gives (month 6+).** Marker that she's bought in.
