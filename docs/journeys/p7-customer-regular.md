# Journey — P7 Customer (CT2 Regular) × Hair

**The cell.** Mary McNamara, 58. Regular customer of Lara at Aurora Studio for 7 years. WhatsApp-first. Never installs apps. Books cuts every 6 weeks; colour every 3 months.

**Why this journey.** The customer-side experience is the make-or-break for adoption — if it feels worse than calling the salon directly, the salon won't switch. The CT2 Regular journey is the most frequent.

## First touch

- Mary doesn't know Aurora has switched providers. From her perspective: she gets a WhatsApp message from Aurora's normal number that reads slightly differently — *"Hi Mary — Aurora here. Just a heads-up: we've upgraded our booking. You'll keep your usual Tuesday slot with Lara, and we now answer the phone faster than ever. Nothing for you to do — back to normal."*
- That's it. No app to install. No login. No data migration on her side.

## First booking under Liv

- Three weeks later, Mary needs to move her Tuesday to Wednesday. She replies on WhatsApp: *"Need to move Tuesday — possible Wed instead?"*
- Liv responds in <30 sec: *"Yes — Lara has Wed 9am, 11:00, or 2pm. Which works?"*
- Mary: *"11."*
- Liv: *"Done. See you Wed at 11. (Same as usual — wallet pass updated.)"*
- Mary thinks: that was easier than calling the salon.

## First voice call experience

- Mary calls the salon (she sometimes prefers voice). Liv answers: *"Hi, you've reached Aurora Studio — this is Liv, the studio's AI receptionist. Mary?"* (caller-id matched).
- Mary: *"Hi Liv. Can I just confirm Wed at 11?"*
- Liv: *"Yes — Wed 11am with Lara. Anything else?"*
- Mary: *"That's all, thanks."*
- Mary mentions to her daughter that night: "they have AI now. It's actually quite nice. It knew it was me."

## First arrival

- At Aurora Studio Wed 10:55. Síobhan (Receptionist) sees Mary's wallet-pass auto-check-in via Bluetooth (configurable). Greets by name.
- Lara is ready. Service goes as normal.
- At checkout, Mary asks Lara about Lara's daughter (Lara mentioned the wedding 6 weeks ago — Lara remembers).
- Books her next slot at the desk (Síobhan via Liv's interface).

## First "Liv knew" moment

- A few months in, Mary's mum's funeral occurs the week before her usual cut. Mary mentions to Lara at her next visit. Lara writes a note in Mary's record (Liv prompts: "save as a reminder for your next conversation?" — Lara approves).
- Six months later, Mary mentions in DM that she might miss her cut due to her mum's anniversary. Lara writes back personally (Liv stays out — never writes in Lara's voice). Mary feels remembered.

## First "Liv was wrong" as customer experience

- Once, Liv mis-handled Mary's reschedule (booked her at 11am when she'd asked for 1pm — Liv mis-parsed). Mary noticed in confirmation; replied "no I said 1." Liv corrected within 90 seconds with apology: *"Mary — sorry, I misread. 1pm with Lara confirmed."*
- Mary thought: humans get things wrong too. She doesn't switch salons.

## First drift attempt (year 2 — hypothetical)

- Mary skips a cycle (8 weeks past her usual 6-week cadence). She's at the CT6 drift threshold.
- Aurora's setting is OFF for re-engagement. Liv flags to Roisín in the weekly digest: "Mary McNamara drifted past her cycle — first time in 7 years."
- Roisín sees, calls Mary herself. Mary's mum is unwell. Roisín understands; offers a home-visit cut from Lara (which Aurora doesn't usually do).
- This is exactly the kind of moment the brand is built on. Liv didn't intervene; Liv surfaced. The Owner did the human thing.

## What Mary never sees

- Liv's CT classification. Mary is "regular" in the system; she doesn't know.
- The pattern detection. The drift threshold. The wallet-pass-aware check-in (it just feels seamless).
- The hand-off mechanics. To Mary, calling the salon and getting Liv first is just... calling the salon.

## Lifecycle moments designed for

- **The transition (week 0 — when salon switches to Liv).** Customer-side comm matters; the right tone here defines whether the customer notices the change for better or worse.
- **The first DM reschedule.** The proof that Liv is faster + warmer than the old SMS reminder system.
- **The first voice call.** The wedge moment for over-50 customers especially.
- **The first "Liv was wrong" the customer notices.** Recovery defines the impression.
- **The first drift moment.** Owner-side flag, owner-side action — Liv stays out.
- **The 7-year-customer-mention.** Liv supports Lara remembering personal details; Liv doesn't insert herself into the relationship.

## Notes for design

- WhatsApp templates need to feel **like the salon, not like a SaaS.** Lara would never start a message with "Greetings valued customer."
- Voice register on the phone needs to be **warmer than competent** — Mary should feel met, not processed.
- The wallet-pass-aware check-in is optional — must be obvious + consent-gated; never feels surveillance-y.
