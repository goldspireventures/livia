# Competitive response wargame — F9

**Status:** F9 (2026-05-07). Sourced from F6 competitive landscape.

## How to read this

Each scenario: probability, impact on us, our move. Probabilities are subjective; revisited quarterly in foundation audit.

---

## Scenario 1 — Phorest ships an AI receptionist next quarter

**Probability:** Medium-high. Phorest has ~10 years of data, a strong incentive to defend, and the engineering capacity. They'll ship something AI-flavoured within 12 months.

**Likely shape:** A conversational add-on on top of their existing booking engine. Bolt-on, not foundational. Likely English-only initially. Likely no per-character agent; likely no audit-log-as-product-surface. Likely priced as upsell.

**Impact on us:** Moderate. Their AI bolt-on validates the category but doesn't displace our wedge. The "voice receptionist" label gets noisier; demos must work harder.

**Our move:**
1. Sharpen the demo's first 90 seconds so the per-character + audit-log + Senior-w-admin differentiators land before the prospect compares with Phorest's bolt-on.
2. Publish the eval comparison (anonymised) showing where the agentic depth differs.
3. Lean harder into the wedge cells Phorest under-serves (chair-rental, multi-brand, Senior-w-admin role). These are structurally hard for Phorest to add (data model debt).
4. Don't compete on chain-rollup feature parity — accept that's their moat and ours is elsewhere.

---

## Scenario 2 — Fresha drops EU pricing to free + payments

**Probability:** Medium. Fresha already has a free + payments model in some markets; expanding aggressively to EU is plausible.

**Likely shape:** Fresha-style free-base + 1.5% payment fee + stronger marketplace push.

**Impact on us:** Moderate-high in the C2 solo + C4 single-shop cells where price-sensitivity is highest. Lower-impact in C5+ (Manager-equipped salons value depth over free).

**Our move:**
1. Lean into customer-belongs-to-the-salon (Bet 5). Fresha's marketplace cut + customer-ownership-via-Fresha is the trade we don't make.
2. Highlight per-character agent + audit log — not features Fresha can charge for in a free model.
3. Don't drop our base price. Compete on what Fresha structurally can't.
4. Voice receptionist remains structurally distinguishing — Fresha's free tier won't cover the per-tenant phone-line cost.

---

## Scenario 3 — Square buys Booksy

**Probability:** Low-medium. Square has acquired before (Afterpay); Booksy fits a vertical-expansion thesis. But Booksy is large enough that price might be prohibitive.

**Impact on us:** Low in EU (Booksy weak EU; Square weak EU). Higher in UK + Nordics where Booksy has presence.

**Our move:**
1. Watch UK + Nordics specifically; if Booksy customers in those markets feel the acquisition disruption, that's a switching window.
2. Build the Booksy CSV importer + concierge migration ahead of expected disruption window.
3. Don't pre-emptively change strategy.

---

## Scenario 4 — Toast adds salons to its vertical list

**Probability:** Low. Toast is restaurant-vertical; salon expansion would be off-strategy.

**Impact on us:** Low if it happens.

**Our move:** Watch but don't react.

---

## Scenario 5 — A new well-funded EU-native AI-first appointment platform launches

**Probability:** Medium. The "AI for appointments" space is attractive to founders + VC. Within 18 months we should expect ≥2 credible competitors emerge.

**Likely shape:** Similar wedge to ours; potentially better-funded; potentially less depth.

**Impact on us:** High. This is the scenario we plan around.

**Our move:**
1. **Speed of category definition.** F7 narrowing (and `livia-positioning.md`, `livia-bets.md`, `livia-manifesto.md`) was done early for this reason — be the one who named the category.
2. **Design-partner network defence.** First-100 partners + their case studies + their referrals are a moat.
3. **Audit-log + eval framework + per-character agent depth.** These are 12+ months of work to replicate; new entrants will ship a chatbot bolt-on, not an operator.
4. **Brand-of-Liv as differentiation.** Liv is a designed character. New entrants will call theirs "AI Assistant" and ship a chatbot. The brand difference is our durable advantage.
5. **EU residency + GDPR + EU AI Act posture.** New entrants will skip this; we lead with it.

## Scenario 6 — Phorest acquires us

**Probability:** Low-medium in years 3–5; higher in years 5–7.

**Impact on us:** Existential question — to address in `docs/company/exit-and-acquirer-thesis.md`.

**Our move:** Build the company so this is *an option*, not the only path. (See exit thesis for detail.)

---

## Scenario 7 — Frontier LLM provider drops API price by 90%

**Probability:** Very high over 24 months.

**Impact on us:** Strongly positive on cost envelope; weakens cost-as-moat for incumbents who used cost as the AI-deferral excuse.

**Our move:**
1. Don't adjust pricing (price is anchored to value delivered, not cost).
2. Accelerate features that were cost-bound (more frequent eval; deeper online evals; voice mode in additional locales).
3. Watch for new entrants enabled by lower cost.

---

## Scenario 8 — EU AI Act enforcement bites a competitor

**Probability:** Medium over 24 months.

**Impact on us:** Strongly positive. Validates the regulatory posture (`regulatory-and-legal.md`).

**Our move:** Publish a "this is how we comply" piece (not gloating; informational). Strengthen the trust-amplification brand.

---

## What this wargame earns us

Pre-decided responses to plausible competitive moves. Less reaction time when they happen. A clearer view of which moves we ignore (Scenarios 3, 4) vs which we plan around (Scenarios 1, 2, 5, 7).

## Cadence

Revisited quarterly in foundation audit. New scenarios added; obsolete ones retired. The wargame is a living doc.
