# Sales battlecard — Livia vs incumbent AI receptionists

**Status:** v1 (2026-05-26) — internal only; approve before customer-facing use  
**Audience:** Founder, sales, Success (positioning only — Support handles objections on tickets)  
**Competitors covered:** Phorest Front Desk AI (Ivy), Fresha AI Concierge

---

## One-line counter

**They added an AI feature to incumbent software. You hired a colleague with an audit trail, role hierarchy, and EU posture — built for appointment-based people businesses they under-serve (solo, chair-rental, multi-brand).**

## When prospect says…

### “Phorest just launched Ivy.”

- Ivy validates the category — good for you.
- Ask: *“Does Ivy know your Manager’s refund cap? Can your auditor read Liv’s diary?”*
- If they’re **solo or chair-rental**, Phorest’s model is not built for them — show chair-host / solo cockpit.
- Do **not** trash Phorest — many shops are happy; sell **structural fit**.

### “Fresha is free and has AI now.”

- Acknowledge economics: you don’t win on monthly fee for price-sensitive solo.
- *“Fresha’s model includes marketplace — do you own your client list?”*
- Voice at scale costs money; Fresha’s free tier does not replicate your **per-tenant audit + policy-bound agent**.
- Migration: Booksy/Fresha import + concierge (design partners).

### “It’s all AI receptionists now.”

- *“Is it a chatbot in the dashboard, or someone who appears in your audit log when she refunds €40?”*
- Demo order: Tuesday briefing → voice sample → **Manager cap** → **audit** (see `sales-motion.md`).

## Demo traps (avoid)

| Trap | Why |
|------|-----|
| Leading with generic chat UI | Looks like Concierge |
| Promising WhatsApp/Instagram before live | marketing-vs-reality |
| Chain rollup vs Phorest | Their moat; yours is elsewhere |
| Fear-based ROI (“€30k lost”) | Violates sales motion |
